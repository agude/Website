History

I was born in California and lived most of my life in the San Francisco Bay Area. I became interested in physics in middle school and decided then that I wanted to study it in college. I attended UC Berkeley from 2004 to 2008, graduating with honors with a B.A. in Physics. While at Berkeley, I worked with Saul Perlmutter and the Supernova Cosmology Project. With the SCP, I wrote an undergraduate thesis on supernova spectra.

In 2009, I began attending the University of Minnesota as a graduate student in physics, with an interest in experimental high energy physics.

Research

I currently work with Jeremiah Mans as a member of the Compact Muon Solenoid Experiment (CMS), a particle detector on the highest energy particle accelerator in the world: the Large Hadron Collider. As part of the collaboration, I have performed worked on the High Level Trigger and Hadronic Calorimeter subsystems. I am also working on two analyses using data from CMS. The first analysis is a measurement of the properties of the Z boson; one of the force carriers of the weak force. Measuring the properties of the Z bosons allows physicists to better understand the interaction of the two protons at the moment of their collision in the middle of CMS. The second analysis is the search for a right handed W boson and heavy neutrino. Finding such a particle would give us clues as to why the weak force has a broken symmetry at low energies; a feature that has been observed but not explained.
